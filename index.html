Sat 16 Aug – Grutas de Tolantongo Day Trip

Daily Map (Meeting Point)

Today's Summary

A long but unforgettable day trip to the stunning thermal hot springs of Tolantongo. The day starts very early with a 5:45 AM meeting time at the Angel of Independence monument. The tour includes breakfast, all your gear for the day, and plenty of time to soak in the cliffside infinity pools (pocitos), explore the warm caves and waterfalls, and relax by the turquoise river. View Detailed Itinerary

5:45 AM: Meet your tour group at the Angel of Independence monument.

Morning: Depart for a 90-minute drive to Actopan for an included breakfast.

Late Morning: Arrive at Tolantongo. You'll be provided with a backpack, towels, phone protector, water, headlamp, and snacks.

Activity 1: Soak in the "pocitos," the naturally warm thermal pools carved into the stunning canyon cliffs.

Activity 2: Spend about two hours exploring the incredible warm-water caves and waterfalls.

Lunch: Enjoy a relaxing meal (not included) and swim by the beautiful turquoise river.

~8:00 PM: Arrive back in Mexico City.

Mon 18 Aug – Souvenirs & Departure

Daily Map

Morning: Last-Minute Souvenirs

Departure

Transfer to the airport. Important: Allow at least 3 hours to get to the airport on a weekday morning, as traffic can be very heavy.

Quick Wins: Pre-Booking Checklist

  • Frida Kahlo Museum: Book tickets online 3-6 weeks in advance. They sell out!
  • Teotihuacán Balloon Tour: Book with an operator that includes the on-site walking tour to simplify the day.
  • National Museum of Anthropology: Buy tickets online to skip the line.
  • Airport Transport: Pre-book a large vehicle with a car seat, or be ready to use DiDi Protect.
  • Dinner Reservations: For popular spots, book for 6:00-6:30 pm to avoid long waits.

Extra Tips for a Smoother Week

  • Altitude & Sun: CDMX is at 7,350 ft (2,240m). Take it easy the first day, keep everyone hydrated (carry electrolyte packets), and apply SPF 50 every morning.
  • Rainy Season: August is the wettest month. Plan outdoor activities for the morning and keep indoor options (museums, cafes) for the afternoon when showers are common (3-5 pm).
  • Mosquitoes: Pack and use a spray with 30% DEET, especially for time near the water in Chapultepec and Xochimilco.
  • Kid-Friendly Food: To balance the delicious vegan meals, consider omnivore-friendly spots like El Califa or the stands in Mercado Roma for classic quesadillas or tacos al pastor.
  • Transport: Install the Didi and Beat ride-hailing apps. For Marie, pack a fold-up Mifold or similar travel car seat as they are not always available.

Enjoy an unforgettable week packed with pyramids, gondolas, kayaks, and tacos!
