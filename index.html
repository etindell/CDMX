Linnea, Sorenne & Marie's

Mexico City Adventure

A Family Itinerary: Pyramids, Spooky Dolls, Tacos & Rock Climbing!

Mon 11 Aug – Late Night Arrival

9:00 PM: Touchdown in CDMX!

Welcome to Mexico City! Tonight is all about getting from the airport to the Camino Real Polanco and checking in. Time to get a good night's sleep to start the adventure tomorrow.

Reminder:

The Churro Making Class can be cancelled for a full refund before today, August 11th.

Tue 12 Aug – Churros & Historic Center

Daily Map

11:30 AM: Churros Master Class

Time to make everyone's favorite treat! Learn how to make authentic churros from a Mexican pastry chef. Marie can help mix the dough! You can find more details here: Churros Master Class.

Afternoon: Zócalo & Templo Mayor

After a delicious lunch of your own making, head to the Historic Center. Explore the massive Zócalo plaza and tell Sorenne the spooky stories of the ancient Aztec temple that was discovered right in the middle of the city!

Reminder:

The Hot Air Balloon tour must be cancelled by today, August 12th, for a full refund.

Wed 13 Aug – Hot-Air Balloon Over Pyramids

5:30 AM: Teotihuacán Experience

The very early start is worth it! You'll be picked up for a coffee break before your 45-60 minute balloon flight over the stunning Valley of Teotihuacán at sunrise. After landing, there's a traditional toast and a full breakfast buffet at a local restaurant. The tour then gives you time to explore the archaeological zone on your own before returning to the hotel. The afternoon is reserved for a long, well-deserved nap! (Note: Entrance to the pyramids is not included). More details at your booking page.

Thu 14 Aug – Chapultepec Park Fun

Daily Map

Morning: Papalote Museo del Niño

Start the day at the incredible Papalote Museo del Niño (Children's Museum). It's full of hands-on science exhibits and has a huge, dedicated toddler zone that Marie will adore. This is a perfect indoor morning activity.

Afternoon: Anthropology & Park Play

After lunch, head to the world-famous Museo Nacional de Antropología. Sorenne will love the giant, mysterious stone carvings! Afterwards, if the weather is clear, enjoy a walk and maybe a row-boat rental for Linnea on Chapultepec Lake.

Fri 15 Aug – Coyoacán Art & Xochimilco Canals

Daily Map

10:15 AM: Frida Kahlo Museum (Order #2366247)

Your timed entry for the iconic Casa Azul is at 10:15 AM. A typical visit lasts 1.5-2 hours, so I recommend planning to leave around 12:00 PM to find a spot for lunch in Coyoacán's beautiful Jardín Centenario.

~1:30 PM: Museo Diego Rivera-Anahuacalli

After lunch, take a short taxi ride to this unique, pyramid-like museum. It's a striking contrast to Casa Azul and showcases Diego Rivera's incredible collection of pre-Hispanic art.

Late Afternoon: Xochimilco Canals

Head to Xochimilco's Embarcadero Las Flores for a 1.5-hour trajinera boat ride. It's more manageable for kids than the long trip to the (very spooky) Island of the Dolls, but you can still hear all the legends from the boatmen!

Sat 16 Aug – Gondolas, Tacos & Markets

Daily Map

Morning: Cablebús Line 3

Get the same gondola thrill with better views and safety on the new Cablebús Line 3. It starts right in Chapultepec, is easy to get to, and offers spectacular views and art at the stations.

Lunch: Por Siempre Vegana

Time for some of the most famous vegan tacos in the city! Stop at the legendary Por Siempre Vegana taqueria in Roma Norte for an unforgettable lunch.

Afternoon: Bazaar Sábado in San Ángel

Browse the famous Bazaar Sábado arts-and-crafts market. A great chance for Marie to practice her Spanish with the friendly vendors while you shop for unique gifts.

Sun 17 Aug – Rock Climbing & Roma

Daily Map

Morning: La Romita Market

Before the gym opens, take a 10-minute walk to the charming La Romita street market for a look at local crafts and maybe a quick taco.

Afternoon: Rock Climbing for Linnea!

Time for an adventure just for Linnea! Head to Adamanta Climbing Gym in Condesa (opens at noon) for some awesome rock climbing and bouldering that she'll love. They have rental shoes and a tots' area.

Dinner in Condesa

After climbing, enjoy a delicious and creative vegan dinner at Veguisima, a local favorite in Condesa, to celebrate the day's adventure.

Mon 18 Aug – Souvenirs & Departure

Morning: Last-Minute Souvenirs

A quick run to Mercado de Artesanías La Ciudadela to find a special souvenir for each of the girls to remember their amazing trip. It opens at 10 am.

Departure

Transfer to the airport. **Important:** Allow at least **3 hours** to get to the airport on a weekday morning, as traffic can be very heavy.

Quick Wins: Pre-Booking Checklist

  • Frida Kahlo Museum: Book tickets online 3-6 weeks in advance. They sell out!
  • Teotihuacán Balloon Tour: Book with an operator that includes the on-site walking tour to simplify the day.
  • National Museum of Anthropology: Buy tickets online to skip the line.
  • Airport Transport: Pre-book a large vehicle with a car seat, or be ready to use DiDi Protect.
  • Dinner Reservations: For popular spots, book for 6:00-6:30 pm to avoid long waits.

Extra Tips for a Smoother Week

  • Altitude & Sun: CDMX is at 7,350 ft (2,240m). Take it easy the first day, keep everyone hydrated (carry electrolyte packets), and apply SPF 50 every morning.
  • Rainy Season: August is the wettest month. Plan outdoor activities for the morning and keep indoor options (museums, cafes) for the afternoon when showers are common (3-5 pm).
  • Mosquitoes: Pack and use a spray with 30% DEET, especially for time near the water in Chapultepec and Xochimilco.
  • Kid-Friendly Food: To balance the delicious vegan meals, consider omnivore-friendly spots like El Califa or the stands in Mercado Roma for classic quesadillas or tacos al pastor.
  • Transport: Install the Didi and Beat ride-hailing apps. For Marie, pack a fold-up Mifold or similar travel car seat as they are not always available.

Enjoy an unforgettable week packed with pyramids, gondolas, kayaks, and tacos!
